About Me:

Madicken Munk

Who I am:

This is a weekly meeting for sharing skills and best practices for scientific computation. Based on The Hacker Within Scientific Computing Group from the University of Wisconsin Madison, a small group of nuclear engineers at UC Berkeley had a couple such meetings in Fall 2013. In spring semester of 2014, this grew into a more structured set of skill sharing sessions for scientific software development (testing, data management, version control, literate programming etc. ) The goal is to learn cool skills and incorporate these practices into our workflows. This meeting would be a great venue for introducing new libraries, showing off useful features of a neutronics code you're using, or bringing up a computational problem you're having.

What I do for work:

Room 190, Doe Library, The University of California - Berkeley

What I do for fun:

Anyone interested in software development best practices is welcome to come to our meetings. Note that the first hour has a particular emphasis on computational nuclear engineering and neutronics analysis best practices. The second hour of the meeting, however, will be domain-inspecific!

Contact Me

Wednesdays at 3:30pm. The meeting should last about two hours. See the blog or the calendar for the topic for this week. To keep up with meetings, you may want to sign up for the listhost.

How:

Participating is really easy.

  • At 3:30pm, there is a short tutorial/discussion of some neutronics topic.
  • Next, at 4:30pm, there is a tutorial/discussion about a purely computational topic. (feel free to only join for this part).
  • Finally, there will be a time for a couple of Lightning Talks, which are 5-10 minute blasts of information about a particular topic or question of interest to the group.
    • To volunteer to give either of these talks, just let the listhost know by email at ucb-hacker-within@lists.berkeley.edu. Note that the lightning talk time is a good way to bring a question to the group. If you have a bug you need help with, here's the place to ask many ears about it at once.
